Tech Tips for New Entrepreneurs in 2024

Managing or creating a successful startup is no easy feat. Whether you’re operating in a physical space, fully online, or hybrid, a relatively small team needs to cover every aspect of the business – from social media to marketing, analytics, and everything in between.

Here are five of the best free startup tools that WE use and recommend to all of our startup clients!

5. Mailerlite for email marketing


With Mailerlite's advanced email marketing features, you can collect subscribers, strengthen customer relationships, automate workflows, and monetize your audience. Create custom forms that match your brand. Use embedded forms and pop-ups to collect subscribers and promotions to spotlight important messages.

4. Tawk.to for customer communication


Monitor and chat with the visitors on your website, respond to support tickets, organize contacts in a CRM, and create a Help Center to empower customers to help themselves. Translated into 45+ languages, tawk.to makes it easy to chat with customers in your native tongue.


3. Slack for instant messaging


While the free version of Slack won’t unlock every feature, it covers most of the things your startup might need in its everyday operations—It creates workspaces so team members can have private group discussions, send messages, and exchange files.



2. Canva for graphic design


With the free version of Canva, user experience teams can choose from ready-to-use templates to create impressive presentations, logos, marketing ads, banner images, business cards, reports, and wallpapers that customers will definitely find professional.



1. Google Workspace for Collaboration


Google Workspace Formerly known as G Suite, it consists of a wide array of apps that promote collaboration and improve productivity, including email, storage, video conferencing, calendars, spreadsheets, and more.
